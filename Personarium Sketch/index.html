Masks can be physical or psychological.

A persona is a psychological mask we wear to navigate the social world.

The skin is the physical mask that the soul wears to have a human experience.

Let me see

Carl Jung describes 12 archetypes as characters with distinct features and personality traits that are recurrent in our stories and myths. These archetypes reside in our collective unconscious and manifest in the personas we create to fulfill our different roles in societies. Persona meant masks or character in latin. Our identity is formed, in part, by the personas we wear. Taking this a step back, our identity is also related to our skin, as this is the first point of contact with the outer world. The skin serves as a kind of mask for our inner self, or our soul if you go deeper. Skin is so flexible and permeable that it can be whatever you want it to be, including rigid and closed. A mask is sometimes given to us by family, society, or circumstance, but we can always choose whether to wear it or not. A skin or a mask is not fixed or permanent; they can be changed, adapted, removed, or replaced. The secret is to never get too attached to any particular skin or mask and consciously decide when to wear one or the other. We hold multiplicity and infinity within us. To express it, all you need is a flexible skin and a collection of masks.
